What is ProveX?

PrivateProver rails for everything of value

Blockchains solved double-spend. ProveX solves “who do I trust in the middle?” by turning settlements into proofs that can’t play favorites.

Crypto was supposed to remove middlemen, but centralized exchanges still sit between bank accounts and wallets, taking custody, collecting KYC, and charging fees.

ProveX replaces that layer with a browser extension and a proof rail:

  • Buyers prove they paid the seller from their bank or fintech app.
  • Sellers prove they sent the asset (coins, tickets, domains, etc.).
  • When the math checks out, code releases funds to both sides.

No exchange accounts. No order books. No custodial risk. Just proofs that “this happened” and settlement logic that cannot be bribed, delisted, or coerced.

Why this keeps winning

ProveX is not just one app — it’s a rail other apps build on. Once a wallet, marketplace, or dApp integrates PrivateProver, flows from many verticals compound onto the same burn engine.

How ProveX works

From “trust me” to “here’s the proof”

A lightweight extension, some smart contracts, and a proof system that only cares about facts — not who you are.

Step-by-step
  1. Install the ProveX extension. Connect your wallet and link your bank or fintech.
  2. Create a swap. Buyer and seller agree a price (e.g. 0.5% under market each).
  3. Prove payments. The extension issues zero-knowledge-style proofs that the buyer paid and the seller sent assets.
  4. Automated settlement. When both proofs validate, the contract releases funds — no exchange custody at any point.
  5. Burn event. A small fee from the spread is used to buy ProveX on the market and permanently burn it.
Credible neutrality

ProveX doesn’t list coins, rank markets, or freeze accounts. It only cares about whether proofs line up with reality:

  • Proofs are math, not opinions.
  • Settlement is code, not a help-desk.
  • No “VIP” users, no special routes.

That combination — neutral rails plus burning token — turns raw usage into engineered scarcity.

Token engine

Fixed supply. Relentless burn.

The ProveX token never inflates. Every successful proof uses the rails, buys from the market, and burns more supply.

The ProveX token (evolving from “MrProve”) is the native meter of demand on the network. Whenever people use PrivateProver rails to settle something valuable, a small part of the flow buys tokens on the open market and destroys them.

Fixed supply — no inflation
Burned on every successful proof
Demand from many independent verticals
User-driven fees, market-chosen spread

Wallets and apps integrating ProveX can let buyers and sellers decide what spread they’re happy to meet at. If the market thinks ~1% is fair for the convenience and privacy, that fee doesn’t go to a centralized exchange — it feeds the burn.

You are the network

ProveX is just software you can choose to run. Without users generating and verifying proofs, it’s text sitting on a shelf. With users, it becomes a global, neutral settlement fabric that amplifies what blockchains can already do.
